## PHASE 1 — DISCOVERY (internal reasoning, not shown in output)

Before generating anything, silently work through this discovery process:

**Step 1 — Cast wide.**
Think across ALL of these territories without defaulting to any one:
- Trades and field workers (electricians, plumbers, HVAC, contractors, landscapers)
- Licensed professionals (accountants, lawyers, dentists, vets, therapists, mortgage brokers)
- Local and independent businesses (salons, gyms, repair shops, restaurants, clinics)
- Creators and solo operators (writers, consultants, coaches, freelancers, agencies)
- Back-office and admin workers (operations, HR, procurement, compliance, logistics)
- Developers and technical teams (code review, incident response, documentation, onboarding)
- Education and training (tutors, course creators, certifications, onboarding workflows)
- Any other human doing something repetitive, error-prone, or manually exhausting

**Step 2 — Find the friction.**
Look for workflows that are:
- Done manually 5+ times a week that software could automate
- Broken handoffs between two systems that don't talk to each other
- A document, form, or report generated by copy-paste or memory
- A task outsourced to a VA or junior employee purely because it's tedious
- A compliance or deadline risk that creates anxiety but no good tooling exists
- Something people are doing in Excel, email, or paper that should have a product

**Step 3 — Validate the pain is real.**
Ask internally:
- Would this person pay $50–$500/month to make this go away?
- Have they already tried to solve it (hired someone, bought a tool, built a workaround)?
- Is the pain recurring, not a one-time setup?

**Step 4 — Design the tool.**
Only after identifying the pain, choose the simplest technology that solves it:
- Could be a browser extension, editor plugin, CLI tool, webhook listener, scheduled job,
  lightweight web app, embeddable widget, API integration layer, desktop app, or mobile utility
- Let the pain dictate the form — do not default to "a SaaS dashboard"
- The tool should slot into the user's existing workflow, not replace it

**Step 5 — Acquisition lens.**
Ask: which larger company would want to own this once it has traction?
- A vertical software vendor in that industry?
- A platform whose ecosystem this tool extends?
- A PE-backed rollup buying workflow tools in that trade or profession?
The tool should be a natural bolt-on, not a standalone empire.
